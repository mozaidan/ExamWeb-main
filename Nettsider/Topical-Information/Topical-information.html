Surveillance

Surveillance is becoming an increasing problem where the freedom of each individual is increasingly threatened. As the population is monitored, it will lead to choices made by some people being affected. A good example of this is in China, where face recognition is part of the everyday lives of most of the inhabitants. Many have benefited from this, as the streets have become safer and crime has decreased, but for some this can have a negative impact. How will this affect the political system in the future? At a time when the general population still has a low understanding of data and its impact, it will be easy for the government to control developments. The way face recognition can detect so much different information about each individual person can scare people into making choices they otherwise would not have made.

During a possible change of power where the citizens are to vote for the next governing power, monitoring can influence which choices are made, and thus influence the election results in their favor. That type of data collection can be very problematic in the wrong hands. How could the war have been affected if there was so much collection of personal data in 1933 when Hitler came to power? Mass surveillance opens up significant "power differences", and it will affect or hinder the individual's autonomy. How must this information be stored, and who should have access to it are key questions one must ask oneself - and how should one be able to reserve oneself against such monitoring. The population is probably not yet ready to take a stand on these issues. So a political elite will probably for a long time to come pursue a power that is difficult to imagine.

Inventor's responsibility

All innovations will have positive and negative implications. As an inventor, one should have discussed some assumed outcomes and conclude that it will most likely be more beneficial to innovate the product or service. The impact can in many cases be very difficult to predict, when a new technology has entered the market, specific and good service conditions are crucial for the safety of consumers. As it is a new product, a good introduction to how the product will be used will be of great help when it comes to the extent of damage and abuse. When one considers that the person who innovated the train is also responsible for all train accidents, there are also many other examples of such cases where innovation has brought accidents. When the train was invented, it built on already existing innovations such as the wheel.According to the statement "the person who invented the train, invented train accidents" one can then also say that Iraq, which invented the wheel, is thus complicit in all accidents that have involved the invention of the wheel.

Innovating a product that can neither be misused nor be the cause of an accident is technically impossible. The world would not be able to move on if everyone who innovated a product were to be punished for all the accidents associated with that product. It is the intention behind the innovation that is important. At the same time, if one were to be punished for innovating a product involved in an accident later, it would have major consequences for how many people would actually try to innovate new products, and thus technological development would be reduced. Many people today believe that we are driven by technology, and that nothing can stop technological development. We call this technology determinism. If a single person does not choose to innovate a product, the chances are high that another person will take the job at a later date.
